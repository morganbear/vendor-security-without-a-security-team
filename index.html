Vendor Security Without a Security Team

A practical way to decide how much vendor scrutiny is enough, without security theater.

The problem this page exists to solve

Small and mid-sized teams often don’t have a security department.

But they still have to decide whether a vendor is safe enough to use.

“How much vendor security review is enough here?”

What’s usually missing isn’t information. It’s a stopping rule:

  • How much scrutiny this vendor deserves
  • Which signals actually matter
  • When you’ve done enough to move forward

What I ended up doing

After repeating the same mental process over and over, I wrote it down.

Not as a compliance framework. Not as a 200-question spreadsheet.

As a practical decision guide: classify the vendor, ask only the right questions for that risk level, interpret answers sanely, and document a defensible decision.

If this sounds familiar

This may be useful if:

  • You’re responsible for approving vendors, but security isn’t your full-time job
  • You’re tired of over- or under-reacting to SOC 2 reports
  • You want something that helps you finish a decision and move on

This is not for enterprise-scale third-party risk programs or full compliance builds.
